A . P . J Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, was a distinguished Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Armed with a background in physics and aerospace engineering, he dedicated over four decades to scientific pursuits at institutions like the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). Recognized as the "Missile Man of India," Dr. Kalam made groundbreaking contributions to ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology. His profound impact extended to India's civilian space program and military missile development, solidifying his status as a national icon. Notably, he played a pivotal role in organizing and overseeing the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, marking a significant milestone in India's defense capabilities since its first nuclear test in 1974. Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam's life exemplifies a remarkable journey of scientific brilliance, leadership, and unwavering dedication to the nation.
